Carbon Reduction Strategies for Your Business


Energy Use Reduction

Upgrade to Energy-Efficient Appliances - Replace older, less efficient appliances with newer, energy-saving models available in Nigeria LED lighting, energy-efficient Heating, ventilation and air conditioning (HVAC) systems, and smart thermostats.

Adopt Solar Energy - Invest in solar panels to reduce dependence on grid electricity and generators. Solar energy is becoming increasingly viable in Nigeria due to abundant sunlight and government support for renewable energy initiatives.

Hybrid Generators - Transition from traditional diesel generators to hybrid solar-diesel generators, which reduce diesel consumption by integrating solar power.

Transportation Optimisation

Optimise Route Planning - Utilise route optimisation software to minimize fuel usage in deliveries and logistics. This can be particularly effective in urban areas where traffic congestion is common.

Adopt Electric Bikes and Tricycles - Consider switching to electric versions which are becoming more available.

Shift to Cleaner Vehicles - Transition to using electric or hybrid vehicles and motorbikes for company operations. Explore partnerships with electric vehicle providers in Nigeria for potential discounts or pilot programs.

Alternative Fuel Use - Transition to alternative fuels like biofuel or compressed natural gas (CNG) for company vehicles and onsite machinery to lower direct emissions from conventional fuel sources.

Preventive Maintenance - Regular maintenance of all vehicles

Sustainable Resource Use

Switch to LPG for Cooking and Heating - Use Liquefied Petroleum Gas (LPG) instead of firewood or charcoal, which are less efficient and more polluting. LPG is widely available and supported by Nigerian energy policies aiming to reduce deforestation.

Biogas for Heat - For SMEs using firewood, consider setting up a biogas digester to convert organic waste into biogas, which can be used for heating and cooking, reducing reliance on firewood.

Maintain Equipment Regularly - Ensure that vehicles and generators are regularly maintained to keep them running efficiently, reducing fuel consumption and emissions.

Air Travel Reduction

Reduce Non-Essential Travel - Limit air travel by adopting virtual meeting technologies. Focus on reducing international flights, which are significant contributors to carbon footprints.

Invest in Carbon Offsets - For essential travel, invest in carbon offset programs that fund renewable energy or reforestation projects in Nigeria, directly contributing to local environmental efforts.

Leverage Local Incentives - Take advantage of Nigerian government incentives for SMEs to adopt sustainable practices. Stay informed about new policies and programs that can support your efforts.
